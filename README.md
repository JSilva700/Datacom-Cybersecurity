# Task 1: Introduction to Cyber Security
Orion Health Services is a mid-sized healthcare technology provider with 250 employees. They manage sensitive patient data and provide cloud-based software to clinics across Australia and New Zealand.

On Monday morning, the IT team noticed unusual outbound traffic from one of their internal servers. By midday, several employees reported being locked out of systems, and a ransom note appeared on the shared drive.

You are a Cybersecurity Analyst Graduate on the internal security team. You’ve been asked to assist in investigating the incident and preparing a report for the executive team.

Incident Type: Ransomware attack 

Initial Entry Point: A phishing email sent to a finance team member, containing a malicious Excel attachment 

Compromised Data: 
- Employee payroll records
- Patient appointment schedules
- Internal system credentials

Systems Affected: 
- File server
- HR and finance systems
- Backup server (partially encrypted)

Indicators of Compromise (IOCs): 
- Suspicious login from an overseas IP
- Use of Mimikatz for credential harvesting
- Encrypted files with .orionlock extension

# Task 2: Cybersecurity risk assessment
As a Cyber Security professional you’ve been asked to review an organization and complete a detailed Risk Report of your findings. 

Client Overview

RetailNova Pty Ltd is a retail company headquartered in Melbourne, Australia. Founded in 2012, the company has grown to employ approximately 1,200 staff and generates an annual revenue of $450 million. RetailNova sells consumer electronics, home appliances, and lifestyle products through both physical stores and a robust e-commerce platform.

The company’s digital infrastructure includes a custom-built e-commerce website and mobile application, integrated with payment gateways PayPal, and Afterpay.

RetailNova uses cloud-connected point-of-sale systems across its 85 stores nationwide. Customer relationship management is handled through Salesforce, while SAP is used for enterprise resource planning, covering inventory, finance, and HR. The company relies on AWS for hosting and it integrates with third-party services for logistics, marketing, and loyalty programs.

RetailNova collects and stores a variety of customer data, including personal information such as names, emails, phone numbers, and addresses. It also retains tokenized payment details, purchase history, behavioral analytics, and loyalty program data.

Employee access to systems is managed through role-based access control (RBAC). Corporate staff are allowed to use their own devices under a BYOD policy and access internal systems remotely via VPN. Internal communication is facilitated through Microsoft Teams and Slack.

RetailNova has experienced several cybersecurity incidents in recent years. In 2023, a phishing attack targeted customer service representatives, resulting in credential compromise. In 2024, a ransomware attempt was thwarted by endpoint protection, though it caused a two-hour downtime. In 2025, a data leak from a third-party marketing vendor exposed the email addresses of 5,000 customers.

The company has implemented basic security awareness training for its staff and uses firewalls and antivirus software.
